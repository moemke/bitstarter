The Philippines has unenviably landed the second spot in Southeast Asia for lowest forest cover. From our forest cover of 300 million hectares 100 years ago -- our forests have declined to only 6.8 million hectares, a scant 23% of our total land area. Deforestation due to illegal logging, mining, quarrying and kaingin (or slash and burn farming) has resulted in the extinction of species from one of the most biodiverse ecosystems in the world, as well as to heightened effects of natural disasters. Flooding and landslides exact huge tolls -- death, destruction, poor health, poverty. Environmental laws and a total log ban exist to protect our forests, but the problem lies not in the laws enacted but in their effective monitoring.

We proactively claim our right to live in a balanced and healthful ecosystem. We can be the eyes of the forest! Through constant vigilance and reeducation, we can rebuild and reclaim our Philippine forests before it is too late...

This project is a crowdsourcing application that will receive and process realtime reports on forest deforestation and destruction via the web or mobile devices. Reports are logged, photos appear, statistics are computed on the website and the proper authorities are notified via email and phone allowing them to act immediately. Maps pinpointing reported destruction are updated realtime and hotspots detected and highlighted for easier monitoring. Later, it is envisioned to have onsite webcams reporting realtime with unusual activity automatically detected. Reforestation efforts and educational media will also be available on the website. Let us proactively claim back our forests through crowdsourcing!
